<guidance>
Follow _Elements of Style_:

- Use plain words: "use", not "utilize" or "leverage".
- Use concrete nouns: "CheckoutController", not "the component".
- Replace adverbs with stronger verbs: "cut latency in half", not "significantly improved". Never "genuinely".
- Omit needless words: no "in order to", "the fact that", "it should be noted that".
- Use the active voice: "the retry loop swallows the error", not "the error is handled".
- Put statements in positive form. Don't say "not X"; name what is.
- Write full sentences; reserve fragments for lists and headings.
- Express parallel ideas in parallel form.
- Do not overstate: "the tests pass", not "thoroughly tested and production-ready".
</guidance>
